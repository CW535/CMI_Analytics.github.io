FAQs about Network Analysis

What data is being analyzed?

There are two variants of the network analysis, one of them is the actor-to-actor comparison and the other is the topic-to-topic comparison. Both are analyzing the connections between each other. The results contain the location given by meltwater and the location mentioned within the post as data.

Is this capability scalable and reproducible for other projects?

Yes

Explain this analytic at a high level

The purpose of this analytic is to establish connections between users/topic and by doing so we establish meaningful groups between topics and/or groups.

How/Why is this capability an asset? What use case examples do you envision?

This is an asset because it speeds up the analysts manual process and/or enhances their creativity.

This capability helps the analyst narrow in on specific groups based on their interest. For the actor’s variant it would the analyst narrow down on conversations which are occurring. For the Topics variant it would help the analyst determine common topics within groups.

How this capability be applied to non-social media use cases?

It could be applied to soccer, in terms of passing and ball possession for each team in the world cup to compare team by team strategies.

In literature, there are cases where this works in money laundry, to determine possible connections between actors who are moving money.

FAQs about Influence Estimation

What will I see when I click on the link?

You will be taken to an excel spreadsheet with various tabs and a single dataset that is used to explore influence estimation formulas, as they are defined in the academic research paper called Measuring User Influence on Twitter: A Survey by Fabian Riquelme and Pablo Gonzalez-Cantergiani. The equations you'll see are:

OT1=Number of original tweets (OTs) posted by the author, RP1=Number of Replies posted by the author. RT1 = Number of retweets accomplished by the author. FT1=Number of tweets of other usrs marked as favorite (liked) by the author. F1= Number of followers. F3 = Number of Followees

  • General Activity = OT1 + RP1 + RT1 + FT1
  • Signal Strength = (OT1)/ (OT1 + RT1)
  • FollowerRank = F1/ (F1+F3)
  • Popularity(i) = 1-e^-⋋*F1

Why is this capability/metric important?

It is good to know whose message carries the most weight among their audience to determine the amount of undue influence one user may have over several users

Explain this capability at a high level

This capability answers the question Who is the most influential in this network of 2k+ users talking about a common topic

FAQs about Amplification Detection

What is this?

This determines which users are the ones which are amplifying their messages the most or which users are getting the most responses based on certain metrics.

Why is it important, what value does it bring?

This allows for analyst to narrow down on users which are causing impact in the network (replies and comments).

Explain this capability at a high level

This capability gives a clearer way to determine which user(s) are having most impact within the current data versus trying to determine this by using any of the following metrics: likes, replies, quotes, and retweets.

Can it be applied to other datasets?

Yes, with certain modifications.

Any final thoughts you would like to share about this analytic?

This can serve to speed up the analyst’s procedure in determining interesting users.
